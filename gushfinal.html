21. Beware of messianic politicians and saviors.

Politicians who claim to be divinely appointed prophets, the chosen one, the only one who can solve the nation's problems, or even God himself, have a quick path to tyranny. Since the nature of divine power is, by definition, impossible to disprove, they can claim to have it and claim it justifies anything they see fit. Those who agree with their ideas and prophecies may give them their unconditional faith, and then it's too late to reverse. Any attempt from that point on to check their "divine" power will be used as an excuse to take more.

Example One: Mussolini

While Mussolini was atheist, he was still able to pose himself as the chosen one in his own way. Most famously, he called himself "Il Duce" (The Leader) as a way of expressing that he was the singular person who could lead Italy to greatness. He also got his religious allies to make the religious connection so he didn't have to: Pope Pius XI called him a "man of Providence," and Cardinal Mercier called him an "envoy of God." Having established himself as the single solution to Italy's problems, he became synonymous with the state itself. So, whenever anyone opposed him, that was unpatriotic opposition against the state, and as he also famously said, "all for the state, nothing outside the state, nothing and no one against the state."

Example Two: Hitler

One thing I should like to say on this day which may be memorable for others as well as for us Germans: In the course of my life I have very often been a prophet, and have usually been ridiculed for it. During the time of my struggle for power it was in the first instance the Jewish race which only received my prophecies with laughter when I said that I would one day take over the leadership of the State, and with it that of the whole nation, and that I would then among many other things settle the Jewish problem. Their laughter was uproarious, but I think that for some time now they have been laughing on the other side of their face. Today I will once more be a prophet: If the international Jewish financiers in and outside Europe should succeed in plunging the nations once more into a world war, then the result will not be the Bolshevization of the earth, and thus the victory of Jewry, but the annihilation of the Jewish race in Europe!

Hitler has repeatedly made messianic claims of himself, as seen in the examples below. The Beer Putsch Trial (of which the first quote below is excerpted) is one example of how Hitler uses his prolific and messianic words to spin attempts to punish him for his crimes into a spectacle that gathers support for him and anger against those who, by prosecuting him, are disagreeing with God. The latter quote shows how he uses the same divine language to suggest the genocide of Jews, placing the blame on fate rather than himself.

The army which we have formed grows from day to day; it grows more rapidly from hour to hour. Even now I have the proud hope that one day the hour will come when these untrained [wild] bands will grow to battalions, the battalions to regiments and the regiments to divisions, when the old cockade will be raised from the mire, when the old banners will once again wave before us: and the reconciliation will come in that eternal last Court of Judgment, the Court of God, before which we are ready to take our stand. Then from our bones, from our graves, will sound the voice of that tribunal which alone has the right to sit in judgment upon us. For, gentlemen, it is not you who pronounce judgment upon us, it is the external Court of History which will make its pronouncement upon the charge which is brought against us. The verdict that you will pass I know. But that Court will not ask of us, ‘Did you commit high treason or did you not?’ That Court will judge us as Germans who wanted the best for their people and their fatherland, who wished to fight and to die. You may pronounce us guilty a thousand times, but the Goddess who presides over the Eternal Court of History will with a smile tear in pieces the charge of the Public Prosecutor and the verdict of this court. For she acquits us.

Example Three: Trump

As we bring this twenty-first lesson into the twenty-first century, while he has not reached remotely close to the levels of power attained by Mussolini or Hitler, he is at least inching closer to tyranny (if he's not already there) and he is a modern example that actually leans more into messianic language. For example, to follow up after he called the pope "weak on crime," he posted an AI-generated image that seemed to most (supporters and opponents alike) like an obvious depiction of himself as Jesus. Despite this offending his supporters, he just said he meant it to be a Red Cross worker (which technically breaks the Geneva Conventions), and so many of his supporters stayed despite their earlier qualms, because if he said it it must be true. When he survived an assassination attempt in Butler, he said "I’d like to think that God thinks that I’m going to straighten out our country," and "I was saved by God to make America great again. I believe that." When the rule of law tries to do its job and prosecute him for his crimes, he just falls back on the faith people have gathered in him: "they want to take away my freedom because I will never let them take away your freedom. They want to silence me because I will never let them silence you." His spiritual advisor had a direct parallel to Jesus: "You were betrayed and arrested and falsely accused. It’s a familiar pattern that our lord and savior showed us."
